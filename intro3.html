Interactivity acts as a symbolic tool just as it does in Inanimate Alice. For example, the cutscene in the introduction which portrays buildings with black windows demonstrates this illusion of control. At first, all the visible windows are black, except for one. When hovering over the black windows, they turn yellow. Yet, clicking these windows will not initiate any action. Only clicking on the window that is already yellow will trigger a response and a transition to the next scene. The illusion of choice is demonstrated by offering the user a veritable sea of windows which they can physically mouse over while only actually allowing them to look into one predefined window, rather than any one they happen to choose. The choice the reader makes to click on the window is really no choice at all, but simply the path that author had already intended for the reader.

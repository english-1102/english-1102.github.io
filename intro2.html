Yet, the affordances of electronic literature still allow symbolism and meaning that simply would not be possible in traditional literature through providing and taking away control . In Inanimate Alice, a story about a young girl searching for her lost father, everything leading up to the climax involves the reader clicking through to advance the story, but the climax itself has no interaction: the story just plays without any user input. This lack of control the reader feels is mirrored perfectly by the lack of control felt by the protagonist as she searches for her father. This is not just some metaphor lost on the casual reader, it is an experience shared by all readers along with Alice herself. This is the power of interactive symbolism: it is not just written on a page, it is fundamentally involved with the reader.

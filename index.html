Controlled of the Uncontrolled

Critical introduction

From basic hyperlinks to first-person video games, interactivity permeates all of digital culture. . Specifically in electronic literature, different forms of interactivity are used to enhance user engagement. Electronic literature was first popularized in 1980 through the use of hyperlinks in which the user writes their own story by navigating through a web of linked text. Next, text adventures, such as “Everybody Dies” became popular. Eventually, these pieces of electronic literature started to resemble games more with graphics and background music in point and click adventures like “RedRidingHood.” Even more recently these pieces of electronic literature have transitioned into full length movies like “Black Mirror: Bandersnatch” where the user can achieve different endings by making different choices. Regardless of the form of interactivity, it allows users to take partial ownership for the piece. Generally, the choices a user makes do not have a significant effect on the story. However, this interaction forces users to think through the different actions a character could make within a given scenario. This makes sure they are actively engaging throughout the entirety of the piece, which in turn increases their understanding of it.

Based on the thorough examination of five pieces of electronic literature, interactivity maintains a user’s attention more than that of an ordinary work of text by allowing them to contribute to a piece’s meaning.

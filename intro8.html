From the hyperlinked text of Twelve Blue to the life-altering decisions of Bandersnatch, each of these five pieces displays the various ways interactivity can be used to enhance the meaning of the piece. Interactivity can add to the meaning of a piece by creating a feeling of emotional responsibility as seen in Inanimate Alice or by piquing the curiosity of the readers as seen in Bandersnatch. Furthermore, interactivity makes sure users are actively involved in the story. By making them constantly think about the narrative, interactivity makes users empathize with the characters and become a part of the story. While users may not actually control the ending, the very act of interaction proves itself a potent tool for electronic storytellers.

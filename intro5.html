Even something as simple as hyperlinks, the original form of interaction with electronic literature, can be surprisingly effective, such as those seen in Twelve Blue by Michael Joyce. Hypertext is defined as when "the author presents a text with a certain number of built-in links, which, when selected, take the reader to a different node of what is in effect a global textual network, for the selected link may lead to the next paragraph or to a document stored on the other side of the world" (Chaouli 602). These hyperlinks break the story down into shorter and more digestible chunks. In Twelve Blue, the user can follow the storyline of any of the major aspects of the piece including love, drowning, friendship, and murder. The unique embedding of the hypertext and the generalization of each major aspect of Twelve Blue on every page contribute to the flow of ideas within, build a complex plot, and allow the user to empathize and become more invested with the text.

However, a user may not have as much control over a story as they initially believe. If a choice is given over two parallel paths a number of times, the number of possible endings increases exponentially. A human programmer, however, will never be able to represent all these possibilities within code. Thus, the author must limit the outcomes in order for the piece to end. When the users fiddle with an electronic work long enough, this restriction on the number of attainable story routes becomes evident.

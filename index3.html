Controlled of the Uncontrolled

Critical introduction

Another example of the lack of control within a story would be in RedRidingHood by Donna Leishman. The interactivity is a symbolic tool as it is in Interactive Alice. For example, one of the scenes in RedRidingHood that demonstrates this illusion of control would be the cutscene in the introduction of a skyline that has buildings with black windows. However, when hovering over the windows they would turn yellow. Within the skyline, there is only one window in one of the buildings that is already yellow. Clicking the other windows would not initiate any action but clicking on the windows that was already yellow would trigger a response and a transition to the next scene. The illusion of choice is demonstrated by making the reader believe that clicking the other windows would progress the story while in reality only one window that was already highlighted resulted in a transition to the next scene. The choice the reader makes to click on the window is the choice that author had already intended for the reader.

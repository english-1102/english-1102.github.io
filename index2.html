Controlled of the Uncontrolled

Critical introduction

Interactivity, just like any other literary element, is a potent symbolic tool. Because of it close connection to control, it allows the author to [insert something] More than other literary elements it engages the reader, making it more power than your run of the mill simile or metaphor. For instance, In the story Inanimate Alice, everything leading up to the climax involves the reader clicking through to advance the story, but the climax itself has no interaction: the story just plays without any user input. This lack of control the reader feels is mirrored perfectly by the lack of control felt by the protagonist as she searches for her lost father. This isn't just some metaphor lost on the casual reader, it's an experience shared by all readers along with Alice herself.
